What is NeuroTrends?

Every year, scientists publish thousands of articles detailing new advances in human brain imaging research, using ever more sophisticated data collection and analysis techniques. But without reading the methods section of all of those articles, there's practically no way to understand the state of the art in neuroscience methods, and whether scientists are making sound methodological choices.

Why does this matter? Reproducibility lies at the heart of scientific progress, and it's almost impossible to achieve reproducible results without understanding the methods that scientists use to uncover new findings. Understanding decisions in research methods is also important for evaluating research quality: Are scientists using the best methods available? Do they choose methods that favor positive results?

NeuroTrends attempts to answer these questions by extracting and analyzing methodological information from thousands of published studies across the functional neuroimaging literature. NeuroTrends currently offers searching articles by methodological choices, visualization of methods trends over time, and extraction of methods information from text.

NeuroTrends is developed and maintained by Joshua Carp.
